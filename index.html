Our mission

Habitat for Nature is a hands-on restoration effort on Bainbridge Island. We are rebuilding forest understory, native plant corridors and wildlife feeding areas so birds, small mammals and pollinators can return, thrive and raise young again.

What began as one backyard is now growing into connected habitat that links forest edges, streams and neighborhood greenspace.

Every tree, shrub and water source is part of a larger story: wildlife coming back where they had almost disappeared.

From brush and ivy to native understory

Clearing invasive plants, replanting native shrubs and keeping food and water available year round has brought songbirds, squirrels and other wildlife back into view.

Projects

Forest edge restorations

Removing invasive blackberry and ivy, then replanting native shrubs, berries and understory trees that provide cover and food.

Backyard to forest habitat

Turning lawns and unused corners into small habitat patches that connect to surrounding forests and greenspace.

Water and feeding stations

Keeping clean water, seed and natural foods available so wildlife can reliably return every day of the year.
