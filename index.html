Our Mission

We are a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization dedicated to restoring and protecting native wildlife habitats.

Our hands-on approach focuses on reintroducing native trees, berries, vines, shrubs, and hazelnuts to rebuild the food web and provide year-round nourishment and shelter. Through education, volunteer work, and community support, we have revitalized our regional temperate forest.

Accomplishments include the return of pollinators such as mason bees, butterflies, and hummingbirds. We’ve also seen a rise in key bioindicators like worms, dragonflies, and frogs, including the endangered spotted frog. These efforts have led to a threefold increase in wildlife activity; bringing back the elusive Douglas squirrel, Steller’s jay, and Nuttall’s rabbit.

Our goal is to expand this success by procuring, restoring and protecting more land to ensure the long-term recovery of native species and their habitats.

Current Projects

Native Plant Expansion

Adding serviceberry, salal, Oregon grape, and hazelnut to bolster year-round forage.

Water & Shelter

Drippers, shallow basins, and brush piles to support birds, squirrels, and pollinators.

Invasive Removal

Removing harmful invasives, such as, ivy and scotch broom; re-establishing native trees, fruit and nut bearing understory, and ground cover.
